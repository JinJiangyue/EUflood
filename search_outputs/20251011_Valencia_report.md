# Flood Event Report: DANA Alice

## 1. Event Overview

On October 11, 2025, the region of Valencia, Spain, including the municipality of Carcaixent, experienced significant disruption due to a severe weather event named "DANA Alice." This event was characterized by intense and persistent rainfall, locally referred to as "lluvia," which led to widespread flooding, or "inundación." The heavy precipitation caused considerable impact, primarily affecting transportation infrastructure within the Valencia province and prompting emergency responses, including mobile alerts in the Alicante region.

## 2. Flood Timeline

*   **2025-10-11 00:00 - 23:59:** The DANA Alice disaster event reached its peak throughout the day, bringing heavy rainfall across the Valencian Community. This resulted in numerous road closures within the Valencia province due to flooding and inundation. Emergency mobile alerts were dispatched to residents in the Alicante region, warning of the severe weather conditions and potential impacts. The worst of the episode was anticipated to bring rainfall accumulations exceeding 140 liters per square meter in some areas [2].

## 4. Impact Assessment

*   **Transport:** Heavy rainfall and subsequent flooding led to significant disruptions in the transportation network. Several roads in the Valencia province were closed. Notably, the CV-525 road, spanning kilometers 0 to 5 between the towns of Alginet and Algemesí, was cut in both directions due to inundation [3].
*   **Economy:** No available data for quantified economic losses at this time.
*   **Safety:** No casualties or injuries have been reported as a direct result of the DANA Alice event.
*   **Response:** Emergency services responded by issuing mobile alert messages to residents in the Alicante region, providing timely warnings about the severe weather conditions [2].

## 5. Summary

The DANA Alice event on October 11, 2025, brought severe heavy rainfall and widespread flooding to the Valencian Community, particularly impacting the Valencia province and prompting alerts in Alicante. While no casualties or quantified economic losses have been reported, the event caused significant disruption to transportation, with key roads like the CV-525 being closed due to inundation. The rapid response, including mobile alerts, underscored the severity of the meteorological phenomenon and the importance of timely public warnings. This event highlights the vulnerability of the region to extreme weather patterns and the need for robust infrastructure and emergency preparedness.
